Sinking of Moskva

Fate of the russian flagship Moskva

Background, in February 2022, the flagship of Russia's Black Sea Fleet, the guided missile cruiser Moskva, left Sevastopol to participate in the 2022 Russian invasion of Ukraine.
The ship was used against the Ukrainian armed forces during the attack on Snake Island. Moskva hailed the island's garrison over the radio and demanded its surrender, receiving the now-famous reply "Russian warship, go fuck yourself" from its garrison. After this, all contact was lost with Snake Island, and the thirteen-member Ukrainian garrison was captured.

The ship was about 65 nmi (120 km) south of Odesa when she was struck by two R-360 Neptune anti-ship missiles.
The Ukrainian missiles were apparently fired from a land-based launcher near Odesa.
Casualties are still a bit unclear, but it seems that around half of the ship´s 500 crew survived.
The sinking of Moskva is the most costly single loss for the Russian military in the war to date, and the ship would cost around US$750 million to replace.
Although Russia did not confirm that Ukrainian missiles had hit the ship, in the morning of 15 April,
Russia launched an apparent retaliatory missile strike against the missile factory Luch Design Bureau in Kyiv, where the Neptune missiles allegedly used in the attack were designed and manufactured.


Situation on April 14

Eastern Ukraine


Russian objective: Capture the entirety of Donetsk and Luhansk Oblasts, the claimed territory of Russia’s proxies in Donbas.
The Ukrainian General Staff reported on April 14 that elements of Russia’s 2nd Combined Arms Army—which was previously withdrawn from the Chernihiv axis—are deploying around Severodonetsk.
This is the first Russian unit withdrawn from fighting in northeastern Ukraine to be recommitted to eastern Ukraine.
These units likely remain degraded, and Russian forces will face challenges integrating units from several military districts into a cohesive fighting force.
Russian forces continued unsuccessful daily attacks against Rubizhne, Popasna, and Marinka and did not make any territorial advances on April 14.
The UK Ministry of Defense reported on April 14 that Russian forces in eastern Ukraine are employing “massive rocket and artillery strikes,”.

Kharkiv and Izyum


Russian objective: Advance southeast to support Russian operations in Luhansk Oblast, and fix Ukrainian forces around Kharkiv in place.
Ukrainian Special Forces destroyed a bridge near Izyum while a Russian military convoy was crossing it on April 13.
Ukrainian Special Forces are likely successfully interdicting Russian operations to reinforce the Izyum axis.
Russian forces launched limited attacks around Izyum in the last 24 hours but did not make any territorial advances.

Southern axis


Russian objective: Defend Kherson against Ukrainian counterattacks.
Fighting continued west of Kherson city on April 14.
Russian forces conducted minor attacks against Ukrainian positions in Oleksandrivka, 30 km west of Kherson city.
Ukrainian forces have destroyed a Russian supply depot in Kherson on April 13.
Heavy Ukrainian shelling of the Choronbaivka airfield.
Ukrainian partisans have been active in the Melitopol region since at least mid-March.

Sumy and northeastern Ukraine


Russian forces continued to redeploy from Belarus to Russia for further deployment to eastern Ukraine.
Elements of the 36th Combined Arms Army of the Eastern Military District are likely redeploying from Gomel Oblast, Belarus, to western Russian Oblasts.
Commercial satellite imagery taken on April 12 confirmed that Russian aircraft have predominantly departed Belarusian airfields for likely redeployment to the Izyum axis or eastern Ukraine.
